Hi, I am Aleksei!

I am a second year Master student at Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology. As well, I am a research assistant at Quantum Communications Group of Russian Quantum Center. My research lies in the field of postprocessing for Quantum Key Distribution — I try to develop novel reconciliation schemes employing polar codes, which are among the latest advances of coding theory.

What will you do after the graduating?

Well, before I joined RQC, I was conducting research with Institute of Information Transmission Problems (IITP RAS) for two years. As a result, I suppose that I am aware enough of what the science is from inside — I like it! — so I would love to become a researcher.

In particular, I am interested in how quantum theory revolutionizes our techonologies or, sometimes, even the global perception of the world. Of greatest attraction for me are quantum computing, quantum information and their practical applications. So, currently I am striving to enroll to PhD on these topics.

Anything else?

Yes, of course! As a side project in my lab, I am also concerned now with SIC-POVMs and quantum state tomography — we try to bring them to life using brilliant IBM Q and Qiskit. Moreover, I sometimes participate in hackathons, and have another interests: music, campus radio, filmmaking. Go further and I will tell you where to read about it.
